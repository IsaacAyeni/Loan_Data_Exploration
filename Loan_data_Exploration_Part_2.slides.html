Effect of Loan Variables on The Borrower Rate and Its Impact on Other Loan Variables¶

by Isaac Ayeni¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I aim to determine the main loan variables that the borrower rate is dependent. Also to be determined is the other variables which are dependent upon the value of the borrower rate.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consists of information regarding 113,937 loans with variables including the borrower rate, loan original amount and other variables all together making up 81 variables. For the purpose of this data exploration and analysis, the number of variables have been reduced to 12 variables and the number of entries have been reduced to 84853 loan entries due to missing information on several variables.

Distribution of Borrower Rates¶

The borrower rates take on values ranging from 0.04 to 0.36. The frequencies across intervals in the borrower rates appear uneven, although there is a notable jump in the frequency occuring a little over 0.3 borrower rate

Distribution of Estimated Loss¶

Estimated loss is the estimated principal loss on charge-offs. The distribution of the Estimated loss show a lot of gaps in the distribution and a significant amount of frequency spikes.

Borrower Rate vs. Estimated Loss¶

On plotting the square-root transformation of the borrower rates against the Estimated loss, an approximately linear relationship is observed between the two variables. From the interrelation between these variables, one may infer that the Estimated loss is directly dependent on the borrower rate

Distribution of the ProsperRating¶

The Prosper Rating is a number assigned at the time a listing was created that represents the rating of a loan by Prosper, with its values ranging from worst at 1 to best at 7. The distribution of the Prosper Rating is roughly unimodal with the score 4 occuring most frequently. On the other hand the majority of the borrowers are employed with a significant portion of them been full-time workers and self-employed

Borrower Rate vs. ProsperRating¶

The borrower rate is negatively correlated to the prosper ratings as the borrower rate tends to drop as the prosper rating go up. The borrower rate can therefore be said to be strongly dependent on the prosper rating

Average Borrower Rate by the Upper Credit Score Range for all Employment Status¶

The upper credit score range is the upper value representing the range of the borrower's credit score as provided by a consumer credit rating agency. The average borrower rates is negatively related to the upper credit score range as the highest upper credit score range end up with the lowest BorrowerRates and the case is the reverse for the lower upper credit score range. Also, the not employed employment status has the greates borrower rate across all upper credit score range values.
